1.
List the Employee Name, their Department Name, and the Location Name for all employees.

Ans SELECT E.employee_name, D.dept_name, D.location_id FROM Employees E inner JOIN Departments D where E.dept_id = D.dept_id;

2.
Retrieve the Project Name, Client Name, and the Total Hours Worked on that project, but only for projects managed by the 'Consulting' department.

Ans select p.project_name, p.client_name, sum(a.hours_worked) as total_hours from projects p inner join departments d on p.dept_id = d.dept_id left join assignments a on p.project_id = a.project_id where d.dept_name = 'Consulting' group by p.project_id, p.project_name, p.client_name;

3.
Find the names of Employees who have worked on a project with Client Name 'GlobalTech Solutions'. List the Employee Name and Project Name.

Ans select e.employee_name, p.project_name from employees e inner join assignments a inner join projects p where e.employee_id = a.employee_id and a.project_id = p.project_id and p.client_name = 'GlobalTech Solutions';

4.
List all Departments that have no Employees currently assigned to them.

Ans select d.dept_name from departments d inner join employees e where d.dept_id = e.dept_id and e.employee_id is null;

5.
Show the Manager Name, the Department Name they manage, and the total number of direct reports they have.

Ans select m.employee_name as manager_name, d.dept_name, count(e.employee_id) as Total from employees m inner join employees e inner join departments d where m.employee_id = e.manager_id and m.dept_id = d.dept_id group by m.employee_id, d.dept_name;

6.
Calculate the Average Hours Worked per Employee across all projects. List the Employee Name and the average hours.

Ans select e.employee_name, avg(a.hours_worked) as avg_hours from employees e inner join assignments a where
 e.employee_id = a.employee_id group by e.employee_id;

7.
Find the Department Name(s) that have an average salary above the company-wide average salary.

Ans select d.dept_name from departments d inner join employees e where d.dept_id = e.dept_id 
group by d.dept_id having avg(e.salary) > (select avg(salary) from employees);

8.
Identify the Client Name(s) whose projects have a total combined hours worked exceeding 500 hours.

Ans select p.client_name,sum(a.hours_worked) from projects p inner join assignments a where 
p.project_id = a.project_id group by p.client_name having sum(a.hours_worked) > 500;

9.
Calculate the total number of employees assigned to each Project, but only include projects that have more than 5 employees assigned.

Ans select p.project_name, count(distinct a.employee_id) as total_employees from projects p inner join assignments a where p.project_id = a.project_id group by p.project_id, p.project_name having count(distinct a.employee_id)>5; 

10.
Retrieve the Department Name(s) that have employees assigned to more than one project.

Ans select distinct(d.dept_name) from departments d inner join employees e inner join assignments a where  d.dept_id = e.dept_id and e.employee_id = a.employee_id group by d.dept_name, e.employee_id having count(distinct(a.project_id)) > 1;

11.
Write a query to list the Employee Name and Project Name for any employee who has worked less than 10 hours on a project.

Ans select e.employee_name, p.project_name from employees e inner join assignments a inner join projects p where  e.employee_id = a.employee_id and a.project_id = p.project_id and a.hours_worked < 10;

12.
Find the Project Name(s) that have no assigned employees.

Ans select p.project_name from projects p inner join assignments a where p.project_id = a.project_id and a.assignment_id is null;

13.
Select the Employee Name(s) whose salary is greater than the maximum salary in their own department.

Ans select employee_name from employees e where e.salary > (select max(salary) from employees where dept_id = e.dept_id);

14.
Assuming the Assignments table has a start_date column, find the Employee Name and their oldest project start date.

Ans select e.employee_name, min(a.start_date) as oldest_start_date from employees e inner join assignments a where  e.employee_id = a.employee_id group by e.employee_id;

15.
Write a MySQL query using the DATE_FORMAT function to list all projects that were active in the year 2023.

Ans select project_name from projects where year(start_date) = 2023;

16.
Find the top 3 Departments based on the total hours worked by all their employees across all projects.

Ans select d.dept_name, sum(a.hours_worked) as total_hours from departments d inner join employees e inner join assignments a where d.dept_id = e.dept_id and e.employee_id = a.employee_id group by d.dept_id order by total_hours desc limit 3;
